The World Food Programme

The World Food Programme is the leading humanitarian organization globally. Operating in 123 countries and territories, their advocacy has always been to save lives and change lives through providing assistance in places riddled by emergencies, and through collaborating with local communities to address nutrition and create resilient environments within their localities.

With over 21,000 staff worldwide, the WFP has continuously worked to combat hunger by distributing emergency food assistance, launching information campaigns against malnutrition, and preventing famine by strengthening education, nutrition, livelihood resilience and social protection systems. The WFP has responded in countless life-saving situations through providing emergency relief, humanitarian support, material and financial support, and strategizing disaster risk reduction. In addition, the organization has endlessly worked with governments to promote nation building and to restore peace in war-riddled territories by settling conflicts and easing tensions in these regions. Aside from these, the WFP has also supported efforts to promote innovation, sustainable livelihoods, climate action, gender equality.

In 2021 alone, WFP aided more than 128 million people through the $9.6 billion they raised in the same year. Among these were the 12.2 million people who benefited from WFP's climate risk management solutions, the 23.5 million people who benefited from WFP's campaigns against malnutrition, the nearly 1 million smallholder farmers who were partnered and assisted, the 8.7 million individuals who received additional food assistance, and a total of 41.8 million individuals that received cash assistance amounting to $2.3 billion.

Throughout its history, the WFP has positively affected the lives of many individuals, and looking forward, will continue to live on to their promise of saving lives and changing lives.


WFP: Their History

1961

THE WFP IS FIRST ESTABLISHED after the proposal of George McGovern to establish a multilateral food aid programme for an initial 3-year period, and became an organization under the United Nations in November. During this period, the WFP provided aid to those affected by major global calamities.

1965

THE WFP IS ENSHRINED FULL-FLEDGE BY THE UN as a program intended to last. Because of this, the programme was extended to accommodate people in need “as long as multilateral food aid is found feasible and desirable”. The WFP continuously expands, launching hundreds and thousands of operations and missions over multiple countries.

2000

WFP INAUGURATES THE FIRST UN HUMANITARIAN RESPONSE FACILITY in Italy that also serves as a strategic warehouse for emergency relief supplies for both international organizations and non-governmental organizations.

2002

THE WFP IS OFFICIALLY THE WORLD'S LARGEST HUMANITARIAN AGENCY with a total of 14,500 employees scattered around the world at the time as certified and recognized by the Guinness World Record.

2005

WFP TAKES OVER THE HUMANITARIAN LOGISTICS FOR ALL UN AGENCIES & NGOS which allows them to coordinate and ensure reliable logistical planning, execution, and support in all their missions across the globe.

2020

THE WFP IS AWARDED THE NOBEL PEACE PRIZE for their efforts in ending hunger, addressing and settling conflicts and violence between parties, and advocating for global issues such as gender equality and climate change.
